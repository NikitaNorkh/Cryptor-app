Online Text Encryption

Encryption is the process of transforming information using special algorithms to make it unreadable to unauthorized persons. It is used in various fields, including:

1. Computer Security: Encryption protects sensitive data like passwords, banking details, etc., from unauthorized access.
2. Communications: Encryption ensures privacy on the Internet by protecting data transmitted via emails, messengers, and other communication channels.
3. Financial Transactions: It secures online payments and banking operations, keeping financial information safe.
4. Military Communications: In defense, encryption plays a crucial role in securing secret information and communications.

There are two main types of encryption:

1. Symmetric Encryption: One key is used for both encryption and decryption. Examples include DES (Data Encryption Standard) and AES (Advanced Encryption Standard).
2. Asymmetric Encryption: Two keys are used: a public key for encryption and a private key for decryption. Examples include RSA and ECC (Elliptic Curve Cryptography).
Typically, encryption involves converting plaintext into an unreadable form using a key or password. Only with the correct key can the information be decrypted, ensuring confidentiality and integrity of the transmitted data.
